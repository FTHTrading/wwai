# TROPTIONS™ Client Presentation Flow

This document describes the recommended sequence for presenting the TROPTIONS platform to sponsors, investors, venue operators, and deployment partners.

---

## Audience: Sponsors

**Goal:** Show them a clear path from package selection to active campaign.

### Recommended Screen Sequence

1. **Homepage** (`/`) — Lead with the value proposition: one platform, one brand, from conversation to paid invoice.
2. **Pricing** (`/pricing`) — Walk through the 5 sponsor tiers. Point out the featured (World Cup) and Enterprise options. Highlight setup fee vs. monthly recurring.
3. **Venue Map** (`/map`) — Show the live operations map. Point out venue categories (stadium, airport, transit, hotel). Explain that every pin is a real activation location.
4. **Campaign Example** (`/campaigns`) — Open a sample campaign. Show the QR codes tab and the performance charts.
5. **Proposal Generator** (`/proposals`) — Create a live proposal: select sponsor, package, venue, term. Watch the contract value calculate in real time.
6. **Billing Dashboard** (`/billing`) — Show the KPI bar and the invoice table. Mention Square, Stripe, and manual payment options.

**Close line:** "Every one of these screens is live. When you say yes, we create your sponsor profile and you're active in less than 7 days."

---

## Audience: Investors

**Goal:** Demonstrate system completeness, revenue model, and scalability.

### Recommended Screen Sequence

1. **Homepage** (`/`) — Open with the STATS bar: 52 routes, 5 tiers, 100% QR attribution, 7-day activation.
2. **Demo** (`/demo`) — Walk through all 7 steps. This communicates the full lifecycle without navigating between pages.
3. **Analytics** (`/analytics`) — Show KPIs. Explain that sponsor revenue, redemptions, and conversion are tracked in real time.
4. **Case Studies** (`/case-studies`) — Walk through the 3 scenarios. Point out the citywide World Cup case: 8 venues, 6-month, $115K contract.
5. **Pricing** (`/pricing`) — Point out the tier structure and contract values. Walk through the add-ons section (custom integrations, white label).

**Key messages:**
- Platform is fully built and operational
- Revenue model is recurring monthly + setup fees
- All data flows through a single unified system
- No per-transaction revenue leakage

---

## Audience: Venue Operators

**Goal:** Show them how their venue benefits from being in the TROPTIONS network.

### Recommended Screen Sequence

1. **Map** (`/map`) — Show where they would appear on the live ops map.
2. **Venues** (`/venues`) — Show an existing venue profile. Click into a venue to show the campaign and QR activity tabs.
3. **Campaign** (`/campaigns`) — Show a campaign running at a venue: QR zone count, scan activity, redemption metrics.
4. **Analytics** (`/analytics`) — Explain that their venue performance is tracked alongside all others.

**Key message:** "You get credited for every scan and redemption that happens at your location. You see the data. Your sponsors see the data."

---

## Audience: Sales Reps / Deployment Partners

**Goal:** Show them the tools they will use daily.

### Recommended Screen Sequence

1. **Dashboard** (`/dashboard`) — Show the daily ops dashboard.
2. **Sponsors** (`/sponsors`) — Create or look up a sponsor.
3. **Proposals** (`/proposals`) — Build a proposal live. Walk through the preview card: package, contract value, included services.
4. **Billing** (`/billing`) — Show how to track invoice status and export CSV.
5. **Launch** (`/launch`) — Show the deployment checklist. Explain what to configure for a new deployment.

**Key message:** "Everything you need to close a deal, run a campaign, and invoice is in one screen flow."

---

## General Presentation Tips

- Always start at the Homepage. Let the brand land first.
- Use the Demo page (`/demo`) when time is short — it covers all 7 steps in one scroll.
- Case Studies (`/case-studies`) are clearly marked as demo scenarios. Use them to frame contract value expectations.
- All data shown is seeded for demonstration. Nothing is fabricated — it's all generated from the same database and logic that runs live campaigns.
