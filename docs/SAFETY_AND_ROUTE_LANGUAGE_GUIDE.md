# Safety and Route Language Guide

This guide is binding for every public surface, sales conversation, recorded demo, marketing asset, and AI response.

## Always use

- **safety-informed** route
- **demo route** (until production data + operator review are in place)
- **operator-reviewed in production**
- **recommended public corridor**
- **verified partner**
- **production requires live data and operator review**

## Never use

- guaranteed safe
- guaranteed secure
- official route
- official partner of <protected brand>
- police-approved
- police-escorted (unless contractually true and currently active)
- secure route
- 100% safe
- exclusive partner of <protected brand> (unless in writing)

## Required disclaimer where any safety route is rendered

> "Safety guidance is informational and demo-based. For emergencies, contact local emergency services immediately."

## Required disclaimer on every demo metric panel

> "Demo data. Production replaces local storage with the database and verified telemetry."

## Imagery rules

- No protected league / team / brand logos unless licensed in writing.
- No images that imply police, military, or official emergency-service endorsement.
- No images of named athletes, performers, or public figures unless licensed.
- Stadium / venue silhouettes are acceptable when generic and unbranded.

## AI concierge (WWAI) rules

- Always responds with informational, non-binding language.
- Never asserts a route is safe — only that it is the *recommended public corridor in our demo dataset*.
- For any urgent safety question, leads with the emergency disclaimer above.
- Never names a specific officer, agency, or response time.
- Never claims real-time data unless the data feed is verified and current.

## When a customer asks for stronger language

Refer them to legal. Stronger language requires a contract clause, an operator review program, and an incident-response runbook.
